# Business Plan Template

## 1. Executive Summary (1 page)
- Business concept and value proposition
- Target market and location advantage
- Financial summary: investment needed, breakeven timeline, projected annual revenue
- Owner's relevant background and why they can execute this

## 2. Market Analysis
- Target market size and demographics (from research data)
- Market trends and growth indicators
- Customer segments with their specific needs
- Demand evidence — cite specific data points from research
- Comparable market examples: what worked/failed in similar contexts elsewhere

## 3. Competitive Landscape
- Direct competitors: names, distances, strengths, weaknesses (from research)
- Indirect competitors and substitutes
- Positioning strategy — how to differentiate specifically
- Pricing strategy relative to competitors (with competitor pricing data)

## 4. Operations Plan
- Daily operations schedule (hour by hour)
- Staffing plan: roles, hiring timeline, compensation, CNSS/benefits
- Key suppliers and sourcing (from local market research)
- Equipment and technology needs (itemized with costs)
- Quality control processes

## 5. Marketing Plan
- **First 100 customers**: Specific acquisition tactics ranked by expected ROI
- Ongoing marketing channels with monthly budget per channel
- Social media strategy specific to the location and demographic
- Delivery platform strategy (if applicable)
- Grand opening / launch plan with timeline

## 6. Financial Projections

### 12-Month P&L (month by month)
Table with columns: Month | Customers/day | Avg Ticket | Revenue | COGS | Rent | Labor | Utilities | Marketing | Other | Net

Account for seasonality: identify peak/slow months from discovery data.

### Cash Flow Projection
When money comes in vs goes out. Show cumulative cash position. Identify the maximum cash need (the deepest negative point before recovery).

### Three Scenarios
- **Base case**: Most likely outcome with stated assumptions
- **Best case (+30%)**: What if demand exceeds expectations
- **Worst case (-30%)**: What if ramp-up is slow, seasonal dip is deeper

### Sensitivity Analysis
What happens if:
- Rent increases 20%?
- Customer volume is 30% lower?
- COGS increases 5% (inflation)?
- Key employee leaves?
- New competitor opens nearby?

## 8. 30-60-90 Day Action Plan

### Days 1-30: Foundation
- Legal setup, lease, permits, registrations
- Key hires (if needed)
- Design and planning

### Days 31-60: Build
- Renovation / fit-out
- Equipment purchase and installation
- Supplier agreements
- Marketing preparation

### Days 61-90: Launch
- Soft opening (invite-only, get feedback)
- Grand opening event
- First month operations and optimization
- Initial metrics tracking
