# Discovery Question Framework

Ask conversationally — 3-4 at a time, adapting follow-ups to what was said.

## Category 1: Property Details (ask first)
1. Exact address or cross-streets (needed for location research)
2. Size (sqm/sqft) and layout — rooms, open floor, storefront, warehouse?
3. Current condition — turnkey, needs renovation, bare shell?
4. Parking — dedicated spots, street, nearby lot?
5. Zoning — commercial, mixed-use, industrial? Restrictions?
6. Lease terms — rent, deposit, duration, escalation, renewal options?

## Category 2: Location & Demographics
7. What's within 500m — anchor businesses, schools, hospitals, transit?
8. Foot traffic patterns — busy/dead times, weekday vs weekend?
9. Neighborhood trajectory — gentrifying, stable, declining?
10. Who lives/works nearby — ages, income, families, professions?
11. **Golden question**: What do locals say is missing? "I wish we had a ___"

## Category 3: Financial Capacity
12. Total available capital — what they CAN invest, not what they "want to spend"
13. Funding source — savings, loan, partner, family? Secured or planning?
14. Monthly runway — how many months can they sustain losses?
15. Existing debt or obligations constraining monthly cash?

## Category 4: Skills & Experience
16. Professional background — what work have they done?
17. Business experience — even informal: side hustles, family business?
18. Skills they enjoy vs skills they hate (sustainability matters)
19. Network — suppliers, partners, potential first customers?

## Category 5: Market Conditions
20. What businesses recently opened nearby? How are they doing?
21. What recently closed? Why?
22. Known upcoming developments — construction, roads, transit?
23. Seasonal patterns — tourism, university calendar, weather?

## Category 6: Regulatory
24. Known licensing or permit requirements?
25. Landlord restrictions on business type?

## Category 7: Personal Goals
26. Income target — minimum acceptable vs aspirational monthly?
27. Involvement — full-time operator, owner-manager, passive investor?
28. Exit timeline — keep forever, sell in 5 years, test for 2 years?

## Handling "I don't know"
- **Critical questions** (address, budget, size): Explain why it matters, suggest how to find out
- **Nice-to-have questions** (seasonal patterns, trajectory): Note gap, move on — research phase fills it
- Never make up answers. Flag unknowns explicitly.
